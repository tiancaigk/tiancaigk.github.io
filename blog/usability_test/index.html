The project is still under development, therefore, only a general framework without details of the test is presented.

Executive summary#

Research objective#

Identify usability issues of the general gameplay experiences.

NB

: Game appreciation is NOT the aim of the current research since usability playthrough and appreciation playthrough would interfere with each other and harm reliability of the entire study.

Key insights#

Categorized by game features, Usability issues and what specifically caused such usability issues were found.

Target user profile#

  • Mix of casual players (weekly gaming time 11-20 hours) and hardcore players (weekly gaming time 30+ hours)
  • At least played one game of the same genre.
  • Familiar with using Xbox controller.
  • N = 8~12

Research methods#

In-game characters/progression were set up prior to the playtest so that players did not have to start from the beginning and would be able to experience main features of the game. Players were informed that they could play the game in a way that they preferred (i.e., following main quests is not a must) for 8 hours.

Oberservation#

Streaming with eye tracking was set up for each player for observation. Players’ behaviors/expressions could tell us some major friction points regarding usability. Ad hoc interview#

During the playtest, players might be intervened and asked to explain what just happened in the game. This was for better understanding the reasons behind the issues that were observed. We could get more reliable answers and avoid false positive (behavior that looks like an issue but is not) by condcuting ad hoc interviews. The interventions were only be performed if

  • information is missed regarding the observation.
  • players showed strong frustration or encountered a walkthrough breaker.
  • players were NOT during a combat or any other intense gameplay.

The interviews were semi-structured and the general interview questions are presented as following:

  1. What are you trying to do?
  2. What are you going to do next?/What do you expect?
  3. What is the blocker here? (if any)
  4. Do you notice the icon/understand that you need to press…? (if it is related to UI/control)
  5. How does this affect you to achieve your objective?
  6. Why did you skip a certain activity? (if any)

My role#

User research moderator

Responsible for: tech set up for the playtest, troubleshooting, observation, conducting interviews, and summarizing the qualitative data.
